Sayadieh: The Signature Traditional Food in Aqaba

Published on December 8, 2025 | Updated February 9, 2026

Aqaba, Jordan's only coastal city, is a vibrant hub where the desert meets the sea. While the city is famous for its Red Sea diving and proximity to Wadi Rum, its culinary identity is rooted in the fresh catch of the day. The signature dish that truly captures the spirit of Aqaba's seaside location is Sayadieh (صيادية), a flavorful and aromatic fish and rice platter that stands as the most iconic traditional food in Aqaba.

More than just a meal, Sayadieh is a celebration of the Red Sea's bounty, a dish that has been perfected by generations of local fishermen and cooks. It is a must-try for anyone looking for an authentic taste of the city and its rich culinary heritage.

1. The Anatomy of Authentic Sayadieh

Sayadieh is a layered dish, both in flavor and presentation. As a staple of traditional food in Aqaba, it consists of three main components that work in harmony:

  • The Fish: Traditionally, a firm white fish like grouper (Najil), sea bass, or cod is used. The fish is often marinated in a blend of lemon, garlic, and local spices, then pan-fried or baked until golden and crispy.
  • The Rice: This is the heart of the dish. The rice is cooked in a rich fish broth and infused with a blend of warm spices, most notably cumin, which gives the dish its characteristic earthy flavor and golden-brown color.
  • The Toppings: The dish is crowned with a generous layer of deeply caramelized onions, which provide a sweet counterpoint to the savory fish and spices. Toasted nuts (pine nuts or almonds) and fresh parsley are often sprinkled on top for texture and color.
Local Tip: The secret to the rice's deep flavor is the caramelized onion broth. The onions are cooked until they are almost black, lending a rich, savory depth to the cooking liquid before the rice is added.
The fresh fish and spices that form the heart of authentic Aqaba Sayadieh.

2. Sayadieh: A Coastal Tradition of Jordan

While variations of Sayadieh can be found across the Levant (Lebanon, Syria, Palestine), the Aqabawi version is distinct, emphasizing the freshness of the Red Sea fish and a generous use of local spices. It is a dish that speaks to the city's history as a vital port and fishing community, making it the definitive traditional food in Aqaba.

The name itself, Sayadieh, is derived from the Arabic word for "fisherman" (sayyad), underscoring its deep connection to the sea. It is a dish often prepared for special occasions, family gatherings, and to celebrate a successful day's catch.

3. Where to Enjoy the Best Traditional Food in Aqaba

To experience the most authentic Sayadieh, you need to go where the locals go. Look for restaurants near the fish market or those that specialize in traditional Aqabawi cuisine.

  • The Fish Market Area: Restaurants in this area often source their fish directly from the daily catch, ensuring the freshest possible ingredients for your meal.
  • Local Homes: The best Sayadieh is often home-cooked. Experiencing this dish in a local home is the ultimate way to appreciate its cultural significance and taste the true traditional food in Aqaba.

4. Pairing and Presentation

Sayadieh is typically served with a side of da'oos (a spicy tomato and chili sauce) and a simple salad or yogurt. The contrast between the warm, spiced rice and the cool, tangy sauce is what makes this traditional food in Aqaba so addictive.

The presentation is grand, with the rice forming a mound on a large platter, the fish placed on top, and the caramelized onions and nuts scattered over the entire dish. It is a communal meal, meant to be shared, reflecting the generous hospitality of the Jordanian people.
