Authentic Ramadan Iftar with a Local Family

Experience the Heart of Aqaba

For tourists visiting Jordan during the holy month, there is no better way to understand the local culture than through a Ramadan Iftar experience. Join our family in Aqaba as we break the fast (Iftar) at sunset. This is not just a dinner; it's an immersive cultural exchange where you'll witness the warmth of Jordanian hospitality firsthand.

Quick Facts

Availability During Ramadan 2026 (Approx. Feb 18 - Mar 18)
Time Starts 30 minutes before sunset (Maghrib)
Duration Approx. 2.5 - 3 Hours
Inclusions Dates & water, traditional soup, main course (Sayadieh or Mansaf), Ramadan desserts (Qatayef), Arabic coffee, and tea.
Price 20 JD per person

What to Expect

  • The Call to Prayer: Experience the magical moment when the Adhan (call to prayer) echoes across Aqaba, signaling the end of the fast.
  • Traditional Breaking of Fast: We begin exactly as tradition dictates—with dates and a glass of water or Laban (yogurt drink).
  • A Feast of Flavors: Enjoy a home-cooked meal featuring Aqaba's signature dish, Sayadieh (spiced fish and rice), or the national dish, Mansaf.
  • Sweet Traditions: No Iftar is complete without Qatayef—traditional Ramadan pancakes stuffed with nuts or cheese.
  • Cultural Dialogue: Ask questions about Ramadan, Jordanian customs, and life in Aqaba in a relaxed, friendly environment.

Why Choose a Local Iftar?

While many hotels in Aqaba offer "Ramadan Tents," a home-based Iftar provides a level of authenticity and personal connection that commercial venues cannot match. You'll sit where we sit, eat what we eat, and leave as a friend of the family.

Pro Tip for Tourists: After Iftar, the streets of Aqaba come alive! We recommend visiting the downtown markets or the Ayla Oasis for the vibrant Ramadan nightlife.
