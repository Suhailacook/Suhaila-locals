Jordan's Historic Journey: The 2026 World Cup Debut

By Suhaila-Locals Team | May 17, 2026

The dream has finally become a reality. For the first time in history, the Jordanian national football team—affectionately known as the Nashama—has qualified for the FIFA World Cup. This monumental achievement has sent waves of joy across the Kingdom, from the bustling streets of Amman to the serene shores of Aqaba.

After a grueling qualifying campaign that saw the team overcome incredible odds, Jordan secured their spot on June 5, 2025, with a convincing 3-0 victory over Oman. Now, as we stand on the cusp of the 2026 tournament hosted by Canada, Mexico, and the USA, the excitement is palpable.

The Challenge: Group J

Jordan has been drawn into Group J, a group that promises both immense challenge and incredible opportunity. The Nashama will face off against the defending champions, Argentina, in what will surely be the most-watched match in Jordanian football history.

Group J Teams Region

Key Players to Watch

Under the leadership of manager Jamal Sellami, the squad is a blend of experienced veterans and rising stars. Keep an eye on Yazeed Abu Laila, whose heroic saves in goal were crucial during the qualifiers, and Yazan Al-Arab, the rock at the heart of the defense.

In midfield, the creativity of Noor Al-Rawabdeh and the tireless work rate of Nizar Al-Rashdan will be essential if Jordan is to navigate this difficult group. The team's spirit, characterized by the "Nashama" identity—bravery and chivalry—will be their greatest asset on the world stage.

A Proud Moment for Jordan

Whether you're watching from a cafe in Aqaba or traveling to the stadiums in the US, this is a moment of national pride. The 2026 World Cup is more than just a tournament for Jordan; it's a testament to years of hard work, passion, and the unbreakable spirit of the Jordanian people.
