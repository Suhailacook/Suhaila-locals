The Ultimate Local's Guide to Things to Do in Aqaba

Published on November 25, 2025

Aqaba, Jordan's only coastal city, is often seen as just a gateway to the Red Sea or a stopover on the way to Petra and Wadi Rum. However, for those who venture beyond the resorts, Aqaba offers a rich tapestry of authentic culture, history, and incredible local experiences.

If you are looking for the true heart of the city, forget the tourist traps. Here is **The Ultimate Local's Guide to Things to Do in Aqaba**, curated to help you discover the hidden gems and genuine hospitality of this vibrant port city.

1. Dive Deep into the Old Town and Local Souks

The most authentic way to experience Aqaba is on foot, away from the modern developments. The old town is a maze of history and commerce, where the pulse of local life beats strongest.

The Souks (Markets): Forget sterile gift shops; the Aqaba souks are where locals shop for everything from fresh produce and spices to gold and traditional clothing. The air is thick with the scent of cardamom, mint, and freshly baked bread. Don't miss the **Spice Souk** for the best Za'atar and the **Fish Souk** to see the day's catch from the Red Sea. This is the perfect place to practice your bargaining skills and interact with local vendors.

Historical Gems: While walking, you can stumble upon sites like the **Aqaba Fort** (Mamluk Castle), which offers a glimpse into the city's strategic past, and the beautiful **Sharif Hussein Bin Ali Mosque**. These sites are often less crowded than the beaches and provide a deep connection to the city's heritage.

Local Tip: The best way to navigate the old town and understand the stories behind the architecture and the people is with a knowledgeable guide. Our Aqaba Walking Tour is specifically designed to uncover these hidden alleys and local secrets, giving you an immersive, authentic experience.

Street Food Delights: Head to the local street vendors for the best falafel, hummus, and the local favorite, **Knafeh** (a sweet cheese pastry). Master the Local Dishes: The ultimate culinary experience is learning to cook the food yourself. Dishes like **Mansaf** (Jordan's national dish) and **Maqluba** (the famous "upside-down" dish) are staples of Jordanian hospitality.

3. Explore the Underwater Wonders of the Red Sea

While the Red Sea attracts many tourists, you can still find unique, local ways to enjoy its beauty without the crowds.

The Coral Reefs: Aqaba is famous for its pristine coral reefs and unique underwater attractions, including the sunken **C-130 Hercules plane** and the **Cedar Pride shipwreck**. While diving and snorkeling are popular, you don't have to get wet to appreciate the marine life.

Glass-Bottom Boat Tours: For a family-friendly and relaxing experience, a glass-bottom boat tour offers a perfect view of the colorful coral and fish. It's a fantastic way to see the vibrant ecosystem of the Aqaba Marine Park.

Local Tip: Look for smaller, locally-operated boats for a more personalized experience. Our Aqaba Glass Boat Tour is a fantastic option that takes you directly over the best reefs and shipwrecks, offering clear views of the Red Sea's hidden treasures.

4. Unwind Like a Local: Evening Activities

After a day of exploring, Aqaba offers a relaxed, family-friendly nightlife.

  • **Walk the Corniche:** Join the locals for an evening stroll along the Corniche (seaside promenade). Grab a fresh juice or a cup of strong Arabic coffee and enjoy the cool sea breeze.
  • **Stargazing:** Due to its clear desert skies, Aqaba and the surrounding areas offer incredible stargazing opportunities. Head just outside the city lights for a truly spectacular view of the night sky.
