Shakira in Aqaba 2026: The Ultimate Local's Guide

Published on February 3, 2026 (Updated February 5, 2026)

The rumors are true—global superstar **Shakira** is coming to Jordan for the first time ever! On **March 28th, 2026**, the Red Sea will echo with the sounds of "Hips Don't Lie" and "Waka Waka" as she performs a historic live concert right here in Aqaba.

Whether you're traveling from Amman, neighboring countries, or flying in from across the globe, this isn't just a concert—it's a chance to experience the magic of Jordan's coastal gem. As locals, we want to make sure your concert weekend is as authentic and delicious as possible.

1. Where to Eat Before the Show

Aqaba's food scene will be buzzing on concert day. Skip the hotel buffets and dive into the real flavors of the city.

  • Pre-Concert Fuel: Grab a quick, world-class falafel sandwich from the downtown souk. It’s light enough to keep you dancing but filling enough to last through the encore.
  • The Local Favorite: For something truly unique, head to Abu Rebhi for their famous fries and eggplant sandwiches. It’s the ultimate Aqaba street food experience.
  • A Celebration Meal: If you're arriving a day early, treat yourself to Sayadieh, our signature spiced fish and rice dish. It's the taste of the Red Sea on a plate.
Local Tip: Want to learn how to make these dishes yourself? Join our Authentic Cooking Class on the morning of the concert! It’s the perfect way to spend your afternoon before heading to the venue.

2. Beyond the Music: Must-See Spots

Don't just come for the music; stay for the magic. If you have a few hours before the gates open, here’s how to spend them:

The Old Town Souk: Take a walk through the spice market. The scents of cardamom and sumac are the perfect sensory introduction to Jordan. It's also the best place to pick up unique, handmade souvenirs.

The Waterfront: Walk the Corniche as the sun sets. The golden hour in Aqaba is spectacular, especially with the anticipation of a major concert in the air.

Local Tip: The best way to see the city's hidden gems is our Aqaba Walking Tour. We'll show you the spots that aren't on the tourist maps, including the best viewpoints for photos.

3. Practical Tips for Concert-Goers

  • Dress for the Desert: March evenings in Aqaba are beautiful but can get a bit breezy by the sea. Bring a light layer for after the show.
  • Stay Hydrated: Try the local Lemon with Mint juice. It’s incredibly refreshing and a Jordanian staple.
  • Book Early: With thousands of fans arriving, hotels and experiences will fill up fast. Make sure to secure your spot for a Traditional Dining Experience to celebrate after the big night!
