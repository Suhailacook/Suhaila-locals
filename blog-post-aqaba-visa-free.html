Local Tip: Entering through Aqaba can save you 40 JOD (approx. $56 USD) per person instantly!

What is the Aqaba (ASEZA) Visa?

The Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority (ASEZA) visa is a special entry permit granted to tourists who enter Jordan through the Aqaba Special Economic Zone. Unlike the standard Jordanian tourist visa, the ASEZA visa is completely free of charge.

How to Qualify for the Free Aqaba Visa

To benefit from this policy, you must enter the country through one of the designated Aqaba entry points:

  • King Hussein International Airport (AQJ): Direct flights into Aqaba are automatically eligible.
  • Aqaba Port: Perfect for those arriving by ferry from Egypt (Nuweiba).
  • Wadi Araba Border: The land crossing from Eilat, Israel.
  • Durra Border: The land crossing from Saudi Arabia.

Jordan Pass vs. Aqaba Visa

If you plan to visit Petra, the Jordan Pass is often a better deal as it includes entry fees. However, if you are primarily visiting Aqaba or have other arrangements, the Aqaba visa-free entry is the most economical choice.
